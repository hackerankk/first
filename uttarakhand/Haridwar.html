Haridwar - The Gateway to God

"Experience divinity along the holy Ganges."

About Haridwar

Haridwar is one of the holiest cities in India, known for its profound religious significance in Hinduism. Situated along the banks of the sacred Ganges River, Haridwar serves as a gateway to the Char Dham Yatra and is a place where millions of pilgrims come to wash away their sins in the Ganges' purifying waters.

Main Attractions in Haridwar

  • Har Ki Pauri: The most famous ghat in Haridwar, where the evening Ganga Aarti draws countless devotees and visitors.
  • Chandi Devi Temple: A revered shrine located on the Neel Parvat hill, accessible by trekking or cable car.
  • Mansa Devi Temple: Situated atop the Bilwa Parvat, this temple is believed to fulfill the wishes of its devotees.
  • Bharat Mata Mandir: A unique temple dedicated to Mother India, showcasing the cultural diversity and patriotism of the nation.

The Ganga Aarti Experience

One of the most unforgettable experiences in Haridwar is the Ganga Aarti held at Har Ki Pauri. This ceremonial worship of the Ganges River is marked by chants, the ringing of bells, and the sight of oil lamps floating on the river. Attending the Ganga Aarti at sunset is a moving experience, bringing devotees closer to divinity and peace.

Things to Do in Haridwar

  • Take a Holy Dip: Pilgrims and visitors alike take a holy dip at Har Ki Pauri, believing it purifies the soul.
  • Temple Visits: Visit the prominent temples, including Mansa Devi and Chandi Devi, both offering panoramic views of Haridwar.
  • Attend Yoga and Meditation Sessions: Several ashrams in Haridwar offer yoga and meditation sessions, perfect for those seeking peace and inner balance.
  • Explore Local Bazaars: The bazaars near Har Ki Pauri offer handicrafts, religious items, and Ayurvedic products, making for a colorful shopping experience.

Best Time to Visit Haridwar

The best time to visit Haridwar is during the cooler months, from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for exploring temples and attending the Ganga Aarti. The monsoon season, from July to September, offers a unique ambiance but may bring heavy rains, while the summer months can be quite hot.
