"Experience peace and adventure along the banks of the sacred Ganges."

About Rishikesh

Rishikesh, located on the banks of the holy Ganges River, is a town renowned for its spiritual heritage and vibrant culture. Known globally as the “Yoga Capital of the World,” it attracts visitors seeking spiritual growth and inner peace. Rishikesh is also a hotspot for adventure sports, combining spirituality with exhilarating experiences.

Main Attractions in Rishikesh

  • Triveni Ghat: A sacred bathing spot where pilgrims gather for the evening Ganga Aarti, an awe-inspiring sight with chants and floating lamps.
  • Lakshman Jhula and Ram Jhula: Iconic suspension bridges over the Ganges offering stunning views and access to temples and ashrams.
  • Parmarth Niketan Ashram: One of the largest ashrams in Rishikesh, known for yoga classes, meditation, and evening Ganga Aarti.
  • Neelkanth Mahadev Temple: A revered Shiva temple situated in the mountains, surrounded by lush greenery and offering scenic views.

Adventure Sports in Rishikesh

Rishikesh is a premier destination for adventure enthusiasts, offering a range of thrilling activities:

  • River Rafting: One of the most popular activities in Rishikesh, with routes on the Ganges offering various levels of rapids.
  • Bungee Jumping: Experience an adrenaline rush by diving from one of India’s highest bungee jumping platforms.
  • Camping and Bonfires: Spend a night under the stars at one of the riverside camps with stunning views and outdoor activities.
  • Cliff Jumping and Trekking: Try cliff jumping into the river and explore scenic treks through the forests around Rishikesh.

Yoga and Spirituality

Rishikesh is globally recognized for its yoga and meditation centers, where visitors can experience peace and learn traditional practices. Numerous ashrams offer short- and long-term courses in yoga, meditation, and wellness, attracting seekers from around the world.

Every year, the International Yoga Festival is held in Rishikesh, bringing together yogis, practitioners, and spiritual teachers from across the globe. This event showcases various yoga forms, meditation techniques, and spiritual discourses, making it a must-attend for those on a spiritual journey.

Best Time to Visit Rishikesh

The ideal time to visit Rishikesh is from February to May and September to November when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The monsoon season, from July to August, brings heavy rainfall, while the winter months offer a serene ambiance for meditation and yoga.
