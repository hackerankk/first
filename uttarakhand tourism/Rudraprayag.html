Rudraprayag - Land of Confluence

About Rudraprayag

Rudraprayag is one of the five sacred confluences of the Alaknanda River and is located at the meeting point of the rivers Alaknanda and Mandakini. It holds significant religious importance for Hindu pilgrims, especially those on the Char Dham Yatra.

The town is named after Lord Rudra, a form of Shiva, and is surrounded by stunning Himalayan views and serene river valleys, making it a spiritual and natural retreat for visitors.

Attractions in Rudraprayag

  • Confluence of Alaknanda and Mandakini: A sacred site where pilgrims worship the confluence of these two holy rivers.
  • Kedarnath: One of the most revered temples of Lord Shiva, Kedarnath is accessible through a trek starting from Gaurikund.
  • Chopta: Known as the 'Mini Switzerland' of India, Chopta offers stunning trekking routes and panoramic views of the Himalayas.
  • Rudranath Temple: A temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, it is one of the Panch Kedar temples and is surrounded by lush green meadows.
